\begin{abstract}
  Reliable broadband connectivity remains a challenge in rural Ireland, terrestrial netowrks deployment is limited by geography and infrastructure cost. This project investigates the potential of Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellite constellationsa as a complmentary brodband solution to existing terrestrial networks. Using orbital porjpagation and modelling, the coverage availability and downlink capacity of LEO constellations will be evaluated over Ireland. The results show how teh differnt designs employed by Kuiper, OneWeb and Starlink drasticly change coverage availability and performance metrics. COmparison with terrestrial 5G highlights the lack of rural coverage and highlights the use case for a complementary system of both LEO constellations and terrestrial networks.
