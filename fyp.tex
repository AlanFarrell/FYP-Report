\begin{abstract}
  Reliable broadband connectivity remains a challenge in rural Ireland, where terrestrial network deployment is limited by geography and infrastructure cost. This project investigates the potential of Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellite constellations as a complementary broadband solution to existing terrestrial networks. Using orbital propagation and modelling, the coverage availability and downlink capacity of LEO constellations are evaluated over Ireland. The results demonstrate how the different designs employed by Kuiper, OneWeb, and Starlink drastically change coverage availability and performance metrics. Comparison with terrestrial 5G highlights the lack of rural coverage and the use case for a complementary system combining LEO constellations and terrestrial networks.
\end{abstract}
